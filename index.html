Program Outcomes & Professional Expectations ▾
This course is structured around real-world impact and professional growth.

By the end of the program, each student is expected to:

• Deliver functional, production-quality software that solves real problems.
• Contribute to collaborative projects that mirror real development teams.
• Demonstrate version control proficiency and professional workflow practices.
• Build a portfolio showcasing deployed, working applications.

Evaluation is based on demonstrated competency, collaboration, execution quality, and the ability to ship real, usable software.

The ultimate objective is not simply course completion, but professional readiness — including opportunities for collaboration, public portfolio exposure, and potential pathways toward gainful employment resulting from meaningful contributions.
